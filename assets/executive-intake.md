# Executive Intake Interview

Use this in the first 30–45 minute meeting with a keynote speaker (or their chief of staff). Listen more than you talk. Capture verbatim where possible.

## Audience
1. Who is in the room? On the stream? On social? Rank by importance.
2. What do they believe about us today? About the category?
3. What do you want them to believe, do, or feel after this keynote?
4. What's the single sentence you want quoted afterward?

## Business stake
5. What changes in our business if this keynote succeeds?
6. What happens if we fumble it?
7. Are there specific metrics (pipeline, share price, recruiting, press coverage) tied to this?

## Message
8. If you could only say three things on stage, what are they?
9. What's the one thing you must NOT say? (Legal, competitive, regulatory, IR sensitivities)
10. Where do you and other executives disagree on the message?

## Story instinct
11. Is there a customer story that captures what we're doing?
12. Is there a moment, a number, a visual, or a demo you'd "die for"?
13. What's the worst keynote you've ever given or watched? Why?
14. What's the best? Why?

## Speaker context
15. How long have you been on this stage / this format before?
16. What's your relationship to the prompter — comfortable or anxious?
17. What's your physical preference — podium, walking, sitting, with a co-host?
18. Are there words / phrases you can't pull off naturally? (We'll cut them.)

## Constraints
19. What's your final hard deadline for content lock?
20. Who has approval rights besides you? Who has veto?

## Producer captures
- Tone, energy, conviction level, default vocabulary, recurring phrases.
- Where the executive's eyes light up — that's where the keynote lives.
- Topics the executive avoids — those are landmines or insecurities; both matter.

## Post-interview deliverable
- Same-day recap email: top 3 takeaways, 3 open questions, proposed next step.
- Within 48h: draft strategy brief and message house v0 for review.
