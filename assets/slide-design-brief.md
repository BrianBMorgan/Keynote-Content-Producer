## Column definitions
- **Assertion headline** — a complete sentence that states the claim. NOT a topic label. "Q4 revenue grew 38%" not "Q4 Results."
- **Evidence** — the visual proof of the claim: chart, photo, screenshot, quote, icon set, customer logo. One claim, one proof.
- **Visual direction** — typographic feel, photographic style, color treatment, motion language.
- **Build** — sequence of in-slide reveals (e.g., bars first, callout second).
- **Transition in/out** — how this slide arrives and leaves (cross-dissolve, hard cut, push, custom motion).
- **Owner** — single name accountable.
- **Due** — date asset is delivered for review.
- **Status** — Draft / In review / Approved / Locked.

## Rules
- **Every title is an assertion.** If you can't write a claim, the slide doesn't earn its place.
- **One claim per slide.** If you have two, you have two slides.
- **No bullet lists of features.** Replace with a visual or split into multiple slides.
- **Text on screen ≠ what the speaker says.** Slides support; they do not duplicate.
- **5:1 visual to text ratio.** If the slide has more text than image, redesign.
- **Test from the back row.** If the assertion is unreadable at 20 feet, type is too small.
- **Color contrast WCAG AA minimum** (4.5:1 for body text).
- **Avoid color-only encoding** in charts.

## Pre-design self-check
- ☐ Headline is a complete sentence
- ☐ One claim only
- ☐ Evidence proves the claim
- ☐ Readable at venue scale
- ☐ Builds support speaker pace, not designer ego
- ☐ Transitions don't fight the script
