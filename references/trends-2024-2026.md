# Trends — 2024 to 2026

Load when modernizing a keynote approach, advising on production choices, or pressure-testing whether a planned show feels current.

## AI in keynote production

**Where it's already standard:**
- **Script drafting & iteration** — fast V0/V1, hate-it-or-love-it drafts, rewording for the ear. The producer's hand still owns final voice.
- **Captioning & translation** — ASR (Whisper, Otter, AWS Transcribe) for live + on-demand; AI translation for non-broadcast tier languages.
- **Image generation** — concept boards, mood frames, B-roll placeholder. Less common as final asset due to brand and IP risk.
- **Voice cloning / synthetic narration** — common for promo and pre-roll, rare for live executive moments.
- **Real-time personalization** — segment-specific stream variants, dynamic captions, attendee-personalized cutdowns.
- **Mood boards, scenic concepts, RFP-stage creative** — AI image and video tools dominate ideation.

**Where it's risky:**
- Synthetic media of executives without ironclad consent and labeling.
- AI-generated factual claims unverified by humans.
- Voice clones used to "fix" live audio (deeply ethically questionable).
- AI-generated customer testimonials (do not do this).

**Producer guardrails:**
- Label AI-generated visuals on-stage if the audience would assume real footage.
- Get written consent for any synthetic likeness or voice.
- Human-in-the-loop on every claim, name, statistic, customer reference.
- Document AI tool usage for legal review.

## Hybrid is not optional anymore

Every flagship keynote now has at least four delivery surfaces:

1. The in-room audience
2. The live stream
3. The on-demand replay
4. The social cutdown ecosystem

Design for all four from the start. Camera capture, ISO recording, caption plan, chapter markers, vertical-aspect cuts — these are Phase 1 decisions, not Phase 8 cleanup.

## Shorter, denser keynotes

Audience tolerance for a 2-hour keynote has collapsed. Even Apple events feel tight at 90 minutes. Pragmatic upper bounds:

- Product launch keynote: 45–75 minutes
- Customer conference flagship: 60–90 minutes
- Investor day keynote segment: 20–30 minutes
- Earnings-adjacent CEO remarks: 8–12 minutes
- Sales kickoff opener: 20–30 minutes

Plan the **density curve** explicitly: where can the audience exhale? A keynote that is 60 minutes of peaks is more tiring than 90 minutes of variety.

## Sustainability as production constraint

- LED reuse > one-off scenic builds.
- Modular stage components that travel across multi-city tours.
- Local crew, local vendors, local fabrication.
- Carbon reporting requested by enterprise clients in RFPs.
- Digital swag / no swag for many corporate events.
- Reduced print, reduced freight.

For the producer: this affects scenic choices, travel for guest speakers, and whether you're shipping a custom build or designing for venue-native gear.

## In-house vs. agency rebalancing

- Many enterprises have built in-house keynote teams (Salesforce, Microsoft, AWS, ServiceNow, F5).
- Agencies are taking on more "experience system" scope — keynote + expo + sponsor activations + post-event content — rather than just keynote production.
- Hybrid models common: in-house keynote producer leads strategy + script, agency leads execution + scenic + technical.

## Demo expectations

- Audiences are demo-literate. They can tell scripted from live, real from staged.
- Multi-modal AI demos (voice, vision, action) are the new bar for tech keynotes.
- Failure on stage is forgiven if framed as honesty ("we're working on this"); cover-ups are punished.

## Investor / analyst keynotes

- Reg FD compliance increasingly producer-visible.
- Safe-harbor statements at the top of any forward-looking section.
- More webcast-first formatting (lower thirds, named speakers, chapter markers from go).
- Investor questions are now routinely AI-translated to global audiences.

## Synthetic media & deepfake risk

- Audiences are wary of AI-generated executives.
- Brand guidelines increasingly mandate disclosure of synthetic content.
- Watermarking and provenance metadata (C2PA) emerging as standard.

## What "modern" looks like in 2026

A keynote that:
- Runs ~60 minutes, not 120.
- Opens with a customer story or a real-world stake, not a corporate sizzle.
- Uses LED canvases for atmosphere, not feature lists.
- Includes one engineered S.T.A.R. moment.
- Demos something real, with a real backup.
- Captions in 3+ languages by default.
- Publishes the on-demand cut within 24 hours.
- Releases at least 10 social cutdowns within 72 hours.
- Reports message pull-through alongside reach.

## What feels dated in 2026

- 90+ minute pace with no audience exhale.
- Bullet-list slides with topic headers.
- "Vision videos" with no narrative tie to the show.
- Demos with no labeled backup.
- Single-language captions on global streams.
- Cutdowns delivered weeks later.
- Stage rebuilt city-by-city without sustainability rationale.
