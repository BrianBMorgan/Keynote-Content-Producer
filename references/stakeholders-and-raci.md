# Stakeholders & RACI

Load when planning approvals, escalations, or cross-functional alignment for a keynote.

## The core cast

### Executives (the speakers)
- **CEO / CMO / CPO / CTO / CFO** — the speakers themselves. They own POV and final delivery.
- **Their chiefs of staff / EAs** — your scheduling lifeline. Befriend them.

### Executive communications
- Owns voice, message discipline, reputation risk, internal-external consistency.
- Often the producer's closest partner for script.

### Product marketing
- Owns positioning, claims, competitive differentiation, launch readiness, demo content.
- The most common source of message bloat. Manage with the message house.

### Product management & engineering
- Owns what is real, what is shipping, what is demo-able vs. concept-only.
- The most common source of last-minute "we can't say that yet."

### Brand & design
- Owns visual identity, look and feel, typography, motion language.
- Partners on slide brief, video treatment, scenic.

### PR & corporate communications
- Owns media strategy, pre-briefs, press release sync, embargoes.
- Will need final claims vocabulary and headline language.

### Investor Relations (IR)
- Mandatory review for any forward-looking statements at investor-adjacent events.
- Reg FD, safe harbor language — non-negotiable.

### Legal & compliance
- Reviews claims, customer story permissions, competitive callouts, IP, music rights, trademark usage, regulatory language.

### Analyst Relations (AR)
- Pre-briefs analysts, manages analyst day overlap, surfaces analyst-sensitive talking points.

### Customer marketing
- Owns customer guest experience, story release approvals, customer brand usage.

### Sales / field marketing
- Cares about pipeline impact, sales-enablement cutdowns, post-event collateral.

### Event ops & global events
- Owns venue, attendee experience, registration, scenic, catering — everything around the keynote.

### Technical production (in-house or agency)
- Show caller, technical director, stage manager, AV crew, scenic, lighting, audio, video, broadcast.

### Agency partners (if used)
- Creative agency, production agency, video house, motion shop, demo development, scenic fabricators.

## Sample RACI for a flagship keynote

R = Responsible (does the work) · A = Accountable (one owner, signs off) · C = Consulted · I = Informed

| Workstream | R | A | C | I |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Strategy brief | Keynote producer | VP Events / Exec Comms | CMO, Product Marketing | Speakers, Brand |
| Message house | Keynote producer | Exec Comms | Product Marketing, PR, IR | Speakers, Sales |
| Story arc / script | Keynote producer | Exec Comms | Speakers, Brand, Product Marketing | Legal, PR |
| Slide design | Presentation design lead | Keynote producer | Brand, Product Marketing | Speakers |
| Video assets | Video producer | Keynote producer | Brand, Product Marketing, Legal | Speakers |
| Demo content | Demo engineer | Product Manager | Keynote producer, Product Marketing | Engineering |
| Customer stories | Customer marketing | Keynote producer | Legal, PR | Sales |
| Run-of-show | Keynote producer | Show producer / Exec Producer | Technical director, Show caller | All crew |
| Cue-to-cue | Show caller | Show producer | Technical director, Operators | Producer |
| Speaker prep | Keynote producer | Exec Comms | Speakers, Chiefs of staff | PR |
| Rehearsals | Keynote producer | Show producer | All speakers, Technical | Exec sponsor |

## Approval gates (the lock points)

Plot these on the calendar working backward from show day:

1. **Strategy brief locked** — T-minus 12 weeks
2. **Message house locked** — T-minus 10 weeks
3. **Outline / beat sheet locked** — T-minus 8 weeks
4. **V1 script draft** — T-minus 6 weeks
5. **Slide V1 / video V1 / demo V1** — T-minus 5 weeks
6. **Script V2 (rehearsal-ready)** — T-minus 4 weeks
7. **Legal / PR / IR review** — T-minus 3 weeks
8. **Final script lock** — T-minus 2 weeks
9. **Asset lock** — T-minus 1 week
10. **ROS lock** — T-minus 5 days
11. **Dress rehearsal sign-off** — T-minus 1 day
12. **Show**

Shorter timelines compress proportionally but the gates remain — skipping legal review to save time is how you end up in the news for the wrong reason.

## Managing executive politics (the unspoken job)

- **Triangulate, don't transcribe.** When two executives give conflicting feedback, don't just relay it — synthesize and propose.
- **Make the audience the third voice in the room.** "From the audience's perspective…" is your most powerful phrase.
- **Bring data, not preferences.** Reference past keynote performance, audience research, competitive positioning.
- **Pre-wire decisions.** Walk into reviews with the decision already aligned with the senior-most stakeholder. Surprises lose.
- **Document agreements.** A short recap email after every review prevents whiplash later.

## Escalation map

- **Story-level conflict** → Exec Comms or CMO.
- **Product claim disputes** → Product Marketing lead, then CPO if unresolved.
- **Legal / regulatory blockers** → Legal, hard stop.
- **Executive availability for rehearsal** → Chief of staff, then CEO sponsor.
- **Budget / scope creep** → Event sponsor + finance.
- **Crew / vendor failures** → Show producer / Executive producer.
- **Show-time emergencies** → Producer decides; debrief after.
