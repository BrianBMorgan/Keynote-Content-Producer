# Measurement — what success looks like

Load when planning KPIs, building a post-event report, or pushing back on a keynote that can't justify its existence.

## Measurement principles

1. **Instrument before you produce.** If you can't define success at Phase 1, you'll fabricate it in Phase 8.
2. **Multiple audiences, multiple metrics.** A flagship keynote has 4–6 distinct audiences (customers, prospects, employees, press, analysts, investors). Each needs its own scorecard.
3. **Distinguish leading and lagging.** Social buzz is leading; pipeline influence is lagging. Both matter, but don't confuse them.
4. **Resist vanity metrics alone.** Views, impressions, and reach are necessary but never sufficient.
5. **Compare to baseline.** A number without context is theater.

## KPI catalog

### Audience reaction
- In-room sentiment (post-event survey, NPS)
- Stream chat sentiment / reactions
- Drop-off rate (where did stream viewers leave?)
- Average watch time (live + on-demand)
- Standing ovations / laughs / gasps (yes, these matter and can be tracked)

### Message pull-through
- Did press use your headline language?
- Did analysts repeat your category framing?
- Did social mentions cluster around the controlling idea?
- Were the message house pillars echoed in customer / partner posts?
- **This is the single most underused KPI.** A keynote that doesn't move language doesn't move belief.

### Share of voice / press
- Volume of coverage vs. prior keynote and vs. competitors
- Tier-1 placement counts
- Sentiment of coverage (positive / neutral / negative)
- Headline accuracy — did press get the message right?

### Social
- Branded hashtag adoption
- Influencer / employee amplification
- Quote tweets / shares of specific moments
- Top 5 clipped moments (what the internet remembered)

### Business impact
- Pipeline influenced (sales attribution to event)
- Demos requested / trials started post-event
- MQL / SQL lift in the week following
- Deal acceleration on existing pipeline
- For investor events: share price reaction in the 24h / 1wk window

### Recruiting & brand
- Career page traffic
- Application volume in following weeks
- Employer brand sentiment

### Asset reuse (the multiplier)
- Number of sales-enablement cutdowns delivered
- On-demand views in the first 30 / 60 / 90 days
- Customer-facing clip embeds
- Internal all-hands / training reuse

### Production-quality KPIs (the producer's scorecard)
- Show ran on time (over/under by minutes)
- Cues missed / called late
- Demo success rate (live vs. backup)
- Rehearsal hours per minute of stage time
- Number of last-week script changes
- Time from show end → cutdowns published

## Post-event report structure

**Executive summary (1 page)**
- Show overview (audience, format, runtime)
- Headline outcomes (3–5 bullets, with numbers)
- Headline risks / learnings (3–5 bullets)

**By the numbers**
- Live attendance / stream peak / on-demand to-date
- Sentiment + NPS
- Press, social, analyst summary
- Pipeline / business impact (caveat lead time)

**Message pull-through analysis**
- Did the controlling idea land? Evidence.
- Pillar-by-pillar pull-through.
- Unwanted narratives that emerged. Why.

**Moment-by-moment**
- Top 5 best-received moments (live + clipped)
- Bottom 3 underperforming moments
- Recommended cutdowns

**Production debrief**
- What worked
- What to change
- What to kill
- Process improvements for next time

**Next-step playbook**
- Cutdown roadmap (clip list, owner, due date)
- Sales enablement plan
- On-demand and SEO plan
- Follow-on content (blog, op-ed, podcast, webinar)

## Cutdown roadmap (the post-event multiplier)

For every flagship keynote, plan at least these cuts:

| Cut | Length | Audience | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Full keynote (on-demand) | Original | All | Owned platform, SEO |
| Highlight reel | 3–5 min | Press, social | LinkedIn, X, YouTube |
| Speaker spotlight | 1–2 min per exec | Recruiting, brand | LinkedIn, internal |
| Customer story spotlight | 2–3 min | Sales | Pipeline acceleration |
| Demo spotlight | 1–2 min | Product marketing | Website, demo requests |
| Moment clips (10–30s) | <30s | Social | Vertical, captioned |
| Quote cards (static) | N/A | Social, sales decks | Asynchronous distribution |
| Sales kickoff cut | 5–10 min | Internal | SKO replay |

Plan all cuts BEFORE the show — they affect camera capture, ISO needs, and music licensing.

## Honest limits of measurement

- Brand impact is real but slow.
- Pipeline attribution to single events is messy; use a hold-out or pre/post comparison.
- Press coverage is a function of news cycle as much as content quality.
- Social numbers reward stunts more than substance — don't optimize for them alone.

A keynote that changes what the audience believes will eventually move the business. Trust the leading indicators and instrument enough to prove it later.
