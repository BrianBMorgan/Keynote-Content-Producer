# Lifecycle — the 8 phases in depth

Load this when you need to know what to do in a specific phase, what to deliver, and what to escalate.

## Phase 1: Strategy & discovery

**Goal:** Define the job the keynote must do before anyone writes a word.

**Activities:**
- Executive intake interviews (use `assets/executive-intake.md`)
- Audience persona review — who is in the room, on the stream, on social
- Business-objective extraction: what changes if this keynote succeeds?
- Competitive / category narrative scan — what is everyone else saying?
- Previous-keynote teardown — what worked, what didn't, what aged badly
- Stakeholder map — who has approval rights, who has veto, who has feelings
- First-pass message hierarchy: must-say / should-prove / parking-lot

**Deliverables:** Strategy brief, message house v0, stakeholder map.

**Failure modes:** Skipping intake. Letting product marketing drive the message house alone. Treating the audience as monolithic.

**Exit criteria:** One controlling idea, 3–5 pillars, named audience belief shift, success metric.

## Phase 2: Narrative & script

**Goal:** Convert the message house into a sequence of audience transformations.

**Activities:**
- Pick a story frame (sparkline, problem/solution, before-after-bridge, what/so what/now what, hero's journey, category shift, customer transformation)
- Beat sheet — every beat has a purpose, an owner, an asset, a timing estimate
- Speaker handoff map — what each speaker inherits, owns, hands off
- Demo insertion plan — where demos land, what they prove
- Identify S.T.A.R. moment(s)
- Draft V1 script in teleprompter format with stage directions and cue callouts

**Deliverables:** Locked outline, V1 script, demo insertion plan.

**Failure modes:** Writing the script before the arc. Letting each speaker write their own section without integration. No tension in the arc.

**Exit criteria:** End-to-end timed read with all transitions written.

## Phase 3: Asset production

**Goal:** Translate the script into every visible and audible asset.

**Activities:**
- Slide briefs with assertion headlines and acceptance criteria
- Video treatments with shot lists, music direction, caption plan
- Motion/LED canvas briefs with technical specs
- Demo briefs with happy path and failure-proofing
- Music cues, walk-on stings, lower thirds
- Version control discipline (filename convention, single source of truth)

**Deliverables:** Brief packets, asset tracker, version log.

**Failure modes:** Skipping briefs and "letting design figure it out." Approving copy-heavy slides. Treating demos as casual.

**Exit criteria:** Every cue in the ROS has a named asset, an owner, and a delivery date.

## Phase 4: Speaker development

**Goal:** Turn the script into credible human delivery.

**Activities:**
- Format script for teleprompter (see `speaker-development.md`)
- Rehearsal cadence: table read → standing read → click-through → demo rehearsal → tech rehearsal → cue-to-cue → dress rehearsal → final polish
- Capture notes in categories: clarity, cuts, pronunciation, slide timing, demo confidence, teleprompter friction, blocking, monitor needs, approvals
- Build Q&A bridges for press / analyst / investor follow-up
- Confidence-monitor content planning

**Deliverables:** Rehearsal-ready script, speaker packet (bios, intros, walk-on music, Q&A bridges), rehearsal notes log.

**Failure modes:** Single rehearsal. Rehearsing without the actual deck. Surprising the executive with new content on show day.

**Exit criteria:** Executive can deliver section under time without prompter without losing the beats.

## Phase 5: Run-of-show & show flow

**Goal:** Build the minute-by-minute operating document.

**Activities:**
- Populate ROS schema (see `assets/run-of-show.md`)
- Derive cue-to-cue for technical operators
- Confirm transitions, mic handoffs, walk-on/off, music cues, lighting looks
- Build contingency branches into the ROS itself (delay package, B-roll, host vamp)

**Deliverables:** Locked ROS, cue-to-cue, contingency map.

**Failure modes:** Verbal-only ROS. Unowned cues. No contingency for delays.

**Exit criteria:** Show caller can run the show from the ROS without asking questions.

## Phase 6: Technical integration

**Goal:** Make the content survive the room.

**Activities:**
- Confirm LED resolution, aspect ratios, safe areas, pixel pitch
- Confirm playback system and asset format (codec, resolution, frame rate, audio)
- Confirm teleprompter rig and font/size
- Confirm captioning, translation, audio description
- Confirm stream graphics and lower-third pipeline
- Build redundancy (backup playback, printed scripts, spare laptops, comms map)

**Deliverables:** Tech spec sheet, redundancy plan, rehearsal sign-off.

**Failure modes:** Discovering aspect-ratio mismatch in tech rehearsal. No redundancy. Stream graphics owned by no one.

**Exit criteria:** Full dress under load completes without intervention.

## Phase 7: Live execution

**Goal:** Deliver the show without losing the story.

**Activities:**
- Green room ops and speaker readiness
- Comms (Clear-Com) channel discipline
- Last-minute script changes routed through producer only
- Save-the-show decisions when something breaks
- Stay calm, stay quiet, stay decisive

**Deliverables:** The show.

**Failure modes:** Producer disappears into the booth. Multiple people pushing changes to the speaker. Panic on intercom.

**Exit criteria:** Show ends on time, on message, on tone.

## Phase 8: Post-event

**Goal:** Convert the show into measurable outcomes and reusable assets.

**Activities:**
- Capture archival masters and ISOs
- Cut social clips, on-demand chapters, sales enablement reels
- Pull metrics: audience reaction, message pull-through, social/SOV, press coverage, pipeline influence, recruiting, share price (for investor-facing)
- Run a structured debrief (what worked / what to change / what to kill)
- Update template library with lessons

**Deliverables:** Recap report, cutdown library, debrief notes, template updates.

**Failure modes:** No debrief. Hero footage with no captions. Cutdowns delivered weeks late.

**Exit criteria:** Stakeholders have what they need to use the keynote for the next 12 months.

## Phase-by-phase escalation rules

- **Phase 1–2:** Escalate when stakeholders cannot agree on the controlling idea. Do not draft until resolved.
- **Phase 3:** Escalate scope creep — new demos, new videos, new speakers — to the executive sponsor with cost.
- **Phase 4:** Escalate executive unavailability for rehearsal. No rehearsal = no lock.
- **Phase 5–6:** Escalate any cue with no owner. Unowned cues fail.
- **Phase 7:** Producer decides in the moment. Escalation is post-show.
- **Phase 8:** Escalate KPI gaps to the sponsor with proposed instrumentation for next time.
